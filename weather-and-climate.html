Weather and Climate

Climate change is a pressing global challenge that affects weather patterns, ecosystems, and human livelihoods. Understanding the science behind weather and climate is crucial for addressing SDG 13: Climate Action.

Introduction

Global warming leads to significant changes in weather patterns, causing severe consequences such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves. This section explores the impacts of climate change and the importance of climate action.

Key Facts

  • Temperature Rise: The Earth's average temperature has risen by approximately 1.1°C since the late 19th century.
  • Extreme Weather: Climate change increases the frequency of extreme weather events.
  • Impact on Biodiversity: Changes in climate threaten various species and ecosystems.
  • Global Response: Countries are called to reduce greenhouse gas emissions under the Paris Agreement.

Challenges of Climate Change

Challenges include increasing temperatures, rising sea levels, and changes in precipitation patterns that disrupt food and water supply.

Solutions and Actions

Global efforts are needed to mitigate climate change impacts, including:

  • Investing in renewable energy.
  • Enhancing energy efficiency.
  • Promoting sustainable agricultural practices.
  • Supporting policies that reduce carbon emissions.
