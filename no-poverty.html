No Poverty

Poverty eradication is one of the biggest global challenges. Sustainable Development Goal 1 aims to end poverty in all its forms by 2030.

Introduction to No Poverty

Globally, more than 700 million people live in extreme poverty, struggling to survive on less than $1.90 a day. Poverty is about more than just income – it affects access to basic services such as education, healthcare, clean water, and decent housing.

Key Facts

  • Extreme Poverty: More than 10% of the world’s population lives in extreme poverty.
  • Vulnerable Populations: Women and children are more likely to suffer from poverty, especially in rural areas.
  • Poverty Rate: The global poverty rate has been cut by more than half since 1990, but the rate of reduction has slowed.
  • COVID-19 Impact: The COVID-19 pandemic pushed an estimated 97 million more people into poverty in 2020.

Challenges to Ending Poverty

Despite the progress made, significant challenges remain in the fight to end poverty:

  • Lack of access to education and job opportunities.
  • Weak social protection systems.
  • Conflict and political instability in many regions.
  • Impact of climate change on vulnerable populations.

Solutions and Actions

Achieving SDG 1 requires global efforts across many sectors. Some solutions include:

  • Investing in education and skills development.
  • Strengthening social protection systems, including healthcare and unemployment benefits.
  • Improving access to basic services like clean water, healthcare, and housing.
  • Supporting economic growth through job creation and fair wages.
