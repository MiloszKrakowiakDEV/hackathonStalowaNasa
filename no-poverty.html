Global Warming and Climate Change

Global warming refers to the increase in the Earth's average atmospheric and oceanic temperatures, primarily due to the emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane (CH₄) resulting from human activities. This phenomenon drives climate change, which manifests through extreme weather events, rising sea levels, disruptions to ecosystems, and animal migrations.

Introduction to Climate Change

Climate change has a broad impact on populations worldwide, particularly on the poorest and most vulnerable communities. Some of the main effects include:

Key Facts

  • Extreme Weather Events: More frequent and intense hurricanes, droughts, and floods.
  • Rising Sea Levels: Estimated sea levels have risen by about 20 cm since the beginning of the 20th century.
  • Water and Agricultural Crises: Changing rainfall patterns threaten food security and access to clean drinking water.
  • Health Threats: Higher temperatures lead to the spread of insect-borne diseases like malaria and dengue.

Challenges of Climate Change

Despite some efforts to combat climate change, significant challenges remain:

  • Inadequate infrastructure to cope with extreme weather.
  • Economic constraints in low-income countries.
  • Political resistance to implementing climate policies.
  • Lack of awareness and education about climate change impacts.

Solutions and Actions

To effectively combat climate change, we must consider a range of solutions:

  • Reducing greenhouse gas emissions through renewable energy sources.
  • Enhancing climate resilience in vulnerable communities.
  • Mobilizing financial resources for climate action, especially in low-income countries.
  • Raising awareness and educating people about climate change and its impacts.

Lesson Plan: Climate Action Through Weather and Climate Studies

Grade Level: High School (Grades 9-12)

Duration: 5 Class Periods (45-60 minutes each)

Objectives:

  • Understand the science behind climate change and its global impact.
  • Analyze local weather patterns and their connection to climate change.
  • Explore NASA data sources and tools to measure climate variables.
  • Develop actionable plans to mitigate climate change at the local level.

Materials Needed:

  • Computers or tablets with internet access
  • Access to NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS)
  • Weather tracking tools (anemometer, thermometer, rain gauge)

Lesson Outline:

Day 1: Introduction to Climate Change and SDG 13

  • Warm-Up Discussion: Student discussions on climate change awareness.
  • Video Presentation: Watch a short video on climate change impacts.
  • Homework Assignment: Research local climate impact reports.

Day 2: Understanding Weather Patterns

  • Review of previous work and a data collection activity using local weather data.

Day 3-5: Project Work

  • Use NASA tools to analyze datasets like land surface temperature (MODIS) and rainfall (GPM).
  • Create a climate action plan based on data findings.

Assessment:

  • Participation in activities and presentations.
